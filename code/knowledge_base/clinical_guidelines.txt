=== Pregnancy ===

Guideline:
Uterine fundal height evaluation is routinely performed during pregnancy to monitor fetal growth and gestational progress.

Guideline:
Prenatal visits commonly include blood pressure monitoring, fetal assessment, maternal health evaluation, and routine pregnancy examinations.

Guideline:
Repeat prenatal assessments within short intervals may be clinically appropriate depending on gestational age and maternal risk factors.

------------------------------------------------------------

=== Hypertension ===

Guideline:
Patients with hypertension commonly receive blood pressure monitoring, medication management, cardiovascular evaluation, and routine follow-up visits.

Guideline:
General health assessments may be appropriate for patients with chronic hypertension when managing long-term care.

------------------------------------------------------------

=== Diabetes ===

Guideline:
Patients with diabetes commonly undergo HbA1c testing, blood glucose monitoring, foot examinations, kidney function evaluation, and routine follow-up care.

Guideline:
Chronic disease management visits are appropriate for diabetes patients.

------------------------------------------------------------

=== Respiratory Disorders ===

Guideline:
Patients with asthma or chronic obstructive pulmonary disease commonly receive pulmonary function testing, respiratory assessment, medication review, and routine follow-up.

------------------------------------------------------------

=== Cardiovascular Disease ===

Guideline:
Patients with cardiovascular disease frequently receive ECG evaluation, blood pressure monitoring, medication management, and follow-up assessments.

------------------------------------------------------------

=== Mental Health ===

Guideline:
Patients with depression or anxiety commonly receive behavioral health assessment, counseling evaluation, medication review, and follow-up visits.

------------------------------------------------------------

=== Musculoskeletal Disorders ===

Guideline:
Patients with arthritis or musculoskeletal disorders commonly receive physical examination, mobility assessment, pain evaluation, imaging when indicated, and rehabilitation follow-up.

------------------------------------------------------------

=== Kidney Disease ===

Guideline:
Patients with chronic kidney disease commonly undergo renal function testing, electrolyte monitoring, medication review, and nephrology follow-up.

------------------------------------------------------------

=== Preventive Care ===

Guideline:
Routine wellness examinations may include health assessments, preventive screening, immunizations, counseling, and care planning.

Guideline:
Assessment of health and social care needs is appropriate when evaluating patient support requirements, chronic disease management, discharge planning, or care coordination.

------------------------------------------------------------

=== Laboratory Services ===

Guideline:
Laboratory investigations should generally be clinically supported by the patient's diagnosis, symptoms, or ongoing disease management.

------------------------------------------------------------

=== Imaging ===

Guideline:
Diagnostic imaging should generally be supported by documented clinical indications and medical necessity.

------------------------------------------------------------

=== Repeat Procedures ===

Guideline:
Repeat procedures performed within 30 days are not automatically inappropriate. They may represent appropriate follow-up care, treatment monitoring, or management of chronic conditions.

------------------------------------------------------------

=== Duplicate Billing ===

Guideline:
Duplicate claims with identical patient, provider, procedure, date, and amount should be reviewed for potential duplicate billing.

------------------------------------------------------------

=== High Cost Claims ===

Guideline:
Claims with unusually high costs compared with similar procedures or provider history should be reviewed for billing accuracy and medical necessity.

------------------------------------------------------------

=== Provider Behavior ===

Guideline:
Providers with unusually high billing volume or consistently abnormal claim patterns may require additional payment integrity review.

------------------------------------------------------------

=== Clinical Consistency ===

Guideline:
Clinical appropriateness should be assessed by evaluating whether the documented condition, procedure, patient demographics, and clinical reason are medically consistent.

------------------------------------------------------------

=== Age and Gender Consistency ===

Guideline:
Pregnancy-related procedures should generally be consistent with patient demographics and documented pregnancy-related conditions.

Guideline:
Age-specific procedures should be evaluated to ensure they are clinically appropriate for the patient's age group.

------------------------------------------------------------

=== Payment Integrity ===

Guideline:
A claim should not be denied solely because it appears unusual. Clinical evidence, financial indicators, provider behavior, and historical patterns should all be considered before making a recommendation.

------------------------------------------------------------

=== Manual Review ===

Guideline:
Claims with conflicting clinical evidence, unusual billing patterns, or multiple moderate-risk indicators should be referred for manual review rather than automatic denial.
